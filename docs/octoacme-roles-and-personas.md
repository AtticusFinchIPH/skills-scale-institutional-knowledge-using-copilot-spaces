# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Discuss feature requirements and acceptance criteria
- **With Project Managers**: Participate in planning and risk identification
- **With QA Engineers**: Collaborate on test strategies and bug resolution
- **With UX/UI Designers**: Review design feasibility and user flow implementation
- **With Business Analysts**: Clarify business requirements and edge cases
- **With Technical Writers**: Provide technical details for documentation

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **With Project Managers**: Align on timelines, priorities, and scope
- **With Developers**: Define acceptance criteria and technical trade-offs
- **With QA Engineers**: Establish quality standards and acceptance criteria validation
- **With UX/UI Designers**: Collaborate on user experience and feature requirements
- **With Business Analysts**: Validate business requirements and use cases
- **With Stakeholders**: Report on progress, metrics, and roadmap adjustments

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Align on scope, priority, and milestones
- **With Developers**: Manage planning, dependencies, and blockers
- **With QA Engineers**: Coordinate testing schedules and release readiness
- **With UX/UI Designers**: Incorporate design review timelines into project plans
- **With Business Analysts**: Ensure requirements clarity and traceability
- **With Technical Writers**: Schedule documentation work into delivery timeline
- **With Stakeholders**: Provide status updates and escalate risks/decisions

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers define and advocate for excellent user experience and interface design throughout the project lifecycle. They translate business requirements and user needs into intuitive, accessible, and visually cohesive interfaces.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and translate findings into design requirements
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Collaborate with product and engineering to ensure design feasibility and consistency
- Participate in design reviews and iterate based on feedback
- Ensure usability, accessibility, and visual standards across all interfaces
- Document design systems and interface guidelines

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered design that meets business goals
- Reduce user friction and support adoption
- Establish consistent design standards across the product
- Enable developers to implement features efficiently

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with stakeholders and team members
- Collaboration with developers on implementation details
- User research findings and design rationale documentation
- Accessibility and usability guidelines

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Understand user needs and business objectives
- **With Developers**: Ensure design feasibility and provide implementation guidance
- **With QA Engineers**: Define design acceptance criteria and usability testing
- **With Project Managers**: Coordinate design review timelines and dependency management
- **With Business Analysts**: Understand user workflows and validate design against requirements

---

## QA Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Engineers design, implement, and execute comprehensive test plans to ensure product quality. They own acceptance criteria validation, advocate for testability, and work closely with developers and product managers to triage and resolve issues.

### Responsibilities
- Design and implement test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Execute manual and automated tests across features and integrations
- Identify, document, and prioritize defects
- Collaborate with developers on bug reproduction and resolution
- Validate fixes and regression test releases
- Advocate for quality standards and testability in design and implementation
- Maintain test documentation and traceability matrices

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Reduce time-to-resolution for bugs
- Increase test coverage and automation
- Enable rapid, confident releases

### Typical Communication
- Test case documentation and execution logs
- Defect reports with clear reproduction steps and screenshots
- Quality metrics and test coverage reports
- Collaboration during sprint planning on testability requirements

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **With Developers**: Review code for testability; collaborate on bug fixes
- **With Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria and define quality standards
- **With Project Managers**: Communicate testing readiness and schedule adjustments
- **With UX/UI Designers**: Test design usability and accessibility compliance
- **With Business Analysts**: Clarify business requirements for test case design

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and the delivery team. They gather, document, and clarify detailed business requirements, ensure alignment with objectives, and maintain traceability throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and gather detailed business requirements
- Document requirements with clear, testable acceptance criteria
- Identify and flag conflicting or ambiguous requirements
- Create use case diagrams, process flows, and requirement traceability matrices
- Validate requirements against business objectives and feasibility
- Support planning and help estimate complexity based on business context
- Participate in retrospectives to identify process improvements

### Goals
- Minimize scope creep and rework due to unclear requirements
- Ensure alignment between business needs and technical delivery
- Reduce misinterpretation and rework cycles
- Enable efficient sprint planning and estimation

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and acceptance criteria
- Process flow diagrams and use cases
- Traceability matrices and scope clarification notes
- Stakeholder alignment meetings and status updates

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Clarify business objectives and user needs
- **With Developers**: Explain business context and help resolve requirement ambiguities
- **With QA Engineers**: Define test scenarios aligned with business requirements
- **With Project Managers**: Flag requirement-related risks and dependencies
- **With UX/UI Designers**: Translate business processes into user workflows
- **With Stakeholders**: Conduct interviews, validate requirements, and provide feedback

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain comprehensive user-facing and internal documentation. They work closely with developers, product managers, and QA teams to ensure accuracy, clarity, and usability of all documentation artifacts.

### Responsibilities
- Create user guides, API documentation, and help content
- Maintain accurate and up-to-date technical documentation
- Collaborate with developers on feature documentation
- Develop onboarding materials and training resources
- Establish documentation standards and style guides
- Participate in product reviews to identify documentation needs
- Support localization and accessibility of documentation

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through clear, comprehensive documentation
- Enable faster user adoption and reduce time-to-productivity
- Maintain single source of truth for product knowledge
- Improve product discoverability through searchable documentation

### Typical Communication
- User guides and release notes
- API and technical reference documentation
- Help content and FAQ articles
- Documentation style guides and standards

### Cross-Functional Interactions
- **With Developers**: Gather technical details and understand implementation for accurate documentation
- **With Product Managers**: Understand feature value and user use cases for documentation focus
- **With QA Engineers**: Validate documentation accuracy and completeness
- **With Project Managers**: Coordinate documentation deliverables with release timelines
- **With UX/UI Designers**: Collaborate on user onboarding flows and help system integration
